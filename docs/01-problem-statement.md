# Reserve-A-Ride — Problem Statement

## 1. Context

Urban commuters in South African cities face a fragmented and unreliable transport ecosystem. Existing options include informal lift clubs, public transport, and ride-hailing services. Each has structural weaknesses that impact affordability, safety, and predictability.

---

## 2. Problem Overview

Current commuter transport systems suffer from:

### 2.1 Pricing instability
- Ride-hailing services use dynamic pricing models
- Commuters cannot predict monthly transport costs
- Drivers cannot reliably forecast income

### 2.2 Informal and unsafe transport networks
- Lift clubs operate without structured systems
- No verification, scheduling, or payment guarantees
- Safety and accountability are inconsistent

### 2.3 Inefficient vehicle utilization
- Many private vehicles operate below capacity during peak commuting hours
- Existing platforms do not optimize fixed-route commuter grouping

---

## 3. Core Problem

There is no structured digital system that:

- Guarantees predictable daily commuter transport on fixed routes
- Ensures stable income for drivers through pre-booked seats
- Enforces capacity-based route activation rules
- Provides secure, escrow-based payment handling
- Supports repeat commuter groups for consistency and efficiency

---

## 4. Target Users

### 4.1 Passengers
- Daily workers commuting between residential areas and business districts
- Require predictable pricing and reliable transport schedules

### 4.2 Drivers
- Independent drivers or small fleet operators
- Require stable income and reduced idle time

---

## 5. Proposed Solution (High-Level)

Reserve-A-Ride is a structured commuter scheduling system that enables:

- Fixed-route transport reservations (seat-based model)
- Subscription-style commuter bookings
- Route activation based on minimum capacity thresholds
- Escrow-based payment protection system
- Repeated commuter grouping for route stability

---

## 6. System Goals

- Provide predictable transport pricing for commuters
- Ensure stable and optimized income for drivers
- Improve vehicle occupancy rates on daily commuter routes
- Reduce informal transport risks through structured digital matching
- Create a scalable commuter logistics platform

---

## 7. Constraints

- System must support fixed-route scheduling (not dynamic ride-hailing)
- Must enforce capacity rules before route activation
- Payments must be held in escrow until trip completion
- System must support repeat commuter matching over time

---

## 8. Non-Goals (Important)

The system does NOT aim to:
- Compete directly with real-time ride-hailing platforms
- Provide on-demand instant rides
- Handle long-distance intercity logistics
- Replace public transport infrastructure

---

## 9. Outcome Objective

The objective of this system is to create a structured commuter transport layer that sits between informal lift clubs and commercial ride-hailing platforms, focusing on predictability, safety, and economic stability for both drivers and passengers.
